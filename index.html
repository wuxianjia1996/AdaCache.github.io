Abstract

Diffusion models possess immense commercial prospect in the field of digital content generation due to their impressive generative capabilities. Nevertheless, the nature of sequential iterative denoising of diffusion models consume substantial amounts of computational resources for inference, leading to high operational costs for popular applications. Existing methods to accelerate diffusion models either require extensive retraining or result in inferior generation quality, demonstrating limited feasibility. Herein, we introduce an adaptive cache (AdaCache) strategy to accelerate the diffusion models in a training-free paradigm. AdaCache utilizes the non-uniform phenomenon of high-level features' similarity between adjacent steps during the denoising procedure, thus an adaptive features caching strategy is adopted to significantly accelerate the generation of diffusion models with negligible degradation of generation quality. In addition to speedup image generation, this unique strategy can also be seamlessly applied to video generation models for acceleration. Both quantitative and qualitative results on image and video datasets demonstrate that AdaCache is the state-of-the-art method compared to existing acceleration techniques.
